# Code of conduct

## Our standard

Project spaces should be useful, professional, and safe for people with different
backgrounds and levels of experience. Participants are expected to:

- Discuss ideas and behavior rather than attacking people.
- Give specific, technically grounded feedback.
- Assume good faith while still addressing safety and impact directly.
- Respect boundaries, privacy, pronouns, and requests to stop an interaction.
- Credit sources and collaborators.
- Avoid harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, doxxing,
  impersonation, and sustained disruption.

Disagreement is welcome. Contempt, intimidation, and exclusion are not.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, project
events, and private communications made in an official project role. It also applies when a
participant publicly represents the project.

## Reporting

Do not post sensitive conduct reports in public issues. A dedicated private conduct intake
channel must be configured before public repository publication. Until then, request a
private channel from maintainer Connor Sanders (`jecsand838`) without including sensitive
details. Security-related conduct reports may use the private vulnerability-reporting route
in `SECURITY.md`.

Once a private channel is agreed, include links or screenshots, the approximate time, people
involved, witnesses, and any immediate safety concern.

Maintainers will limit information to people who need it for assessment. Complete
confidentiality cannot be guaranteed, but unnecessary disclosure is not acceptable.

## Enforcement

Responses are proportional to severity, pattern, intent, and impact. They may include:

1. A private clarification or correction.
2. A formal warning with required behavior changes.
3. Temporary restrictions on participation.
4. Removal of content or privileges.
5. A permanent ban.
6. Referral to a platform provider or appropriate authority where credible safety threats
   exist.

A maintainer named in a report must recuse from handling it when another qualified maintainer
is available. Retaliation against a reporter, witness, or reviewer is itself a violation.

## Appeals

A person subject to a restriction may submit one concise appeal identifying a factual error,
a disproportionate response, or new information. Appeals do not suspend urgent safety
measures.
